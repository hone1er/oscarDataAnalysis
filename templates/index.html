Introduction:

The Oscars (also confusingly referred to as the Academy Awards) is a big awards ceremony in the eyes of Hollywood. Winning an Oscar is a sign of high status and recognition. How does one achieve such an award and is the academy fully transparent about the path to this esteemed recognition? We are here to find out. In this project we will be diving into the various factors that go into being nominated. We will first look at what the Academy states is the way to become nominated and then, as data geeks, we will hunt for the data to back-up or debunk the claims.

Our datasets:

https://www.kaggle.com/sebask/the-oscars-dataset-preparing-for-2017/data https://www.kaggle.com/tmdb/tmdb-movie-metadata/data We will be using the above data sets (along with web scraping to gather years 2016-2019) to predict the best picture nomination.

We will be using the following:

HTML/CSS to display our project, Python's Pandas library to web scrape/clean our data Tableau to interactively visualise our data. Further ideas we have are taking a look at each movies instagram followers, Youtube trailer plays, and Netflix streams to see how they trend throughout the year and whether or not there’s a correlation between the top trenders and the nominees. (i.e. how has social media changed the nomination game?)

How this is valuable:

We will be able to show which aspects are the most valuable in predicting who will get nominated for a Oscar. Being nominated for an Oscar can make a huge difference in the financial prospects of the directors, actors, and everyone involved. With the understanding that we unearth, those in the movie business will be able to be more strategic with their resources because they’ll understand what will get them the most bang for their marketing buck.
