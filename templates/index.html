    INTRODUCTION:

    The Oscars (also confusingly referred to as the Academy Awards) is a big awards ceremony in the eyes of Hollywood. Winning an Oscar is a sign of high status and recognition. How does one achieve such an award and is the academy fully transparent about the path to this esteemed recognition? We are here to find out. In this analysis we will be diving into the various factors that go into being nominated. We will first look at what the Academy states is the way to become nominated and then, as data geeks, we will hunt for the data to back-up or debunk the claims.

    DATASETS:

    Dataset 1 Dataset 2
    We will be using the above data sets (along with web scraping to gather years 2016-2019) to predict the best picture nomination.

    TOOLS:

    Python's Beautiful Soup and Pandas library to web scrape/clean our data and Tableau to interactively visualise our data. Further ideas we have are taking a look at each movies instagram followers, Youtube trailer plays, and Netflix streams to see how they trend throughout the year and whether or not there’s a correlation between the top trenders and the nominees. (i.e. how has social media changed the nomination game?)

    VALUE:

    We will be able to show which aspects are the most valuable in predicting who will get nominated for a Oscar. Being nominated for an Oscar can make a huge difference in the financial prospects of the directors, actors, and everyone involved. With the understanding that we unearth, those in the movie business will be able to be more strategic with their resources because they’ll understand what will get them the most bang for their marketing buck.

    RULES:

    All eligible motion pictures must be:

    • Feature length (defined as over 40 minutes)
    • Publicly exhibited by means of 35mm or 70mm film, or in a 24- or 48-frame progressive scan Digital Cinema format
    • For paid admission in a commercial motion picture theater in Los Angeles County for a qualifying run of at least seven consecutive days
    • released within the Awards year deadlines
    • Films that, in any version, receive their first public exhibition or distribution in any manner other than as a theatrical motion picture release will not be eligible for Academy Awards in any category.
